Eye Redness: causes, symptoms, treatment and medicines

Your guide to understanding and treating red eye effectively.

üîç What Is Eye Redness?

Eye redness occurs when blood vessels in the sclera (the white part of your eye) become inflamed or enlarged, leading to a pink or red appearance. It's one of the most common eye complaints and can signal both minor irritation and serious health conditions.

üî¥ Symptoms of Eye Redness

  • Itching or burning
  • Watery or dry eyes
  • Blurred vision
  • Discharge
  • Light sensitivity
  • Pain or discomfort

Fact: According to the National Eye Institute, 25% of adults experience red eyes monthly, often due to screen exposure or allergies.

üß™ Common Causes of Eye Redness

1. Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

Inflammation caused by viral, bacterial or allergic reactions. Contagious when viral or bacterial.

2. Dry Eyes

Common in digital screen users and elderly. Results in reduced tear quality or quantity.

3. Allergies

Triggered by pollen, dust, pets‚Äîoften seasonal.

4. Blepharitis

Chronic inflammation of eyelid margins with crusting and redness.

5. Uveitis

Potentially vision-threatening inflammation inside the eye.

6. Subconjunctival Hemorrhage

Bright red blotch from a burst blood vessel, typically harmless.

7. Glaucoma

Emergency condition marked by severe pain and pressure‚Äîneeds urgent care.

üíä Common Medicines

  • Artificial Tears (e.g., Systane, Refresh) - OTC
  • Naphazoline (Clear Eyes) - OTC
  • Olopatadine (Pataday) - OTC/Prescription
  • Tobramycin, Ciprofloxacin - Prescription
  • Prednisolone Acetate - Prescription
  • Timolol, Brimonidine - Glaucoma meds

Note: Avoid long-term use of redness-relief drops due to rebound effects.
